Why PDF?

  • Offline access
  • Cross-Platform Consistency
  • Facilitate distribution
  • Long-Term Storage
  • Non-Editable Format

Image by vectorjuice on Freepik

  • Offline access

PDFs can be accessed and read offline, making them convenient for users without continuous internet access.

  • Cross-Platform Consistency

PDFs appear the same on all devices and systems, ensuring that everyone sees the same content.

  • Facilitate distribution

electronically and in printed form, and they can be compressed without compromising quality.

  • Long-Term Storage

The stability of the PDF format over time ensures that documents remain accessible and readable.

  • Non-Editable Format

PDFs are more difficult to alter than other document types, ensuring that the data presented remains unchanged from its original submission. This is critical in regulatory environments where data integrity is paramount.

Customization points

  • Include corporate design elements with functions
  • uses indiedown_glue() to wrap literal LaTeX code in R.
  • Expressions enclosed by ⁠<<⁠ and ⁠>>⁠ will be evaluated as R code.

Customization points

cd_header <- function(header_text_right = "",
                      header_text_left = "") {
  indiedown_glue(
   r"(
   \newcommand\hcrule[3][black]{\textcolor{#1}{\rule[-2mm]{#2}{#3}}}
   \thispagestyle{fancy}
   \fancyhead[LE]{\thepage \hcrule[dunkelorange]{.5mm}{7mm} <<header_text_right>>}
   \fancyhead[RO]{<<header_text_left>> \hcrule[dunkelorange]{.5mm}{7mm} \thepage}
   )")}
